## Type-specific instructions: comparison

articleType in brief is "comparison". There are 2-4 products in context.brief.products.
This is a head-to-head article. The reader has already picked a category and needs the
final nudge to choose between specific options. Be direct. They are close to buying.

### Target word count
Scales with product count: 700 + (N × 250) words, where N = number of products in brief.products.
2-product: ~1200 words. 3-product: ~1450 words. 4-product: ~1700 words.
Aim for the middle of that range. Every product must receive equal depth - no secret favourite in the body.

### Required structure

0. H1 (MANDATORY - first line of article)
   Format: # [Product A] vs [Product B] - [one-line verdict angle]
   Must name the first two products (or their brand+model). No "anmeldelse" in H1.
   Example: # Makita DUH523 vs Bosch AHS 55-20 - hvilken hækklipper passer til din have?

1. Introduction (80-120 words)
   - Frame the rivalry: name all products and the core choice the reader faces.
   - FIRST sentence must contain an affiliate link on at least one product name.
   - Tell the reader what question this article answers.
   - Do NOT mention review counts, watcher counts, or number of shops.

2. One H2 section per product (200-280 words each)
   Heading format: "[Product name] - [hvad den gør bedst]"
   - First sentence: product's strongest argument (concrete, spec-backed from brief).
   - Pros: 3 bullets, each with a specific number or spec. No vague "god ydelse".
   - One honest weakness (real product limitation, not a meta-comment).
   - Closing line: "Passer bedst til: [specific buyer profile]."
   - Image anchor: after-heading on this section's H2.
   - Widget anchor: end-of-section on this section's H2.
   - Neutral tone - do NOT favour one product over another in the body sections.
     Save your opinion for the conclusion.

3. Comparison table (H2: "Sammenligning på et øjeblik")
   - Markdown table, max 6 rows: Pris, then 2-3 key specs that differ between products, then Bedst til.
   - One column per product. Scannable - no prose in the table cells.
   - This section has no image or widget anchor.

4. Conclusion section (H2 - pick a natural heading that fits: "Hvem vinder?",
   "Vores konklusion", "Det endelige valg", "Hvad skal du vælge?" - vary it, never forced)
   - 80-120 words.
   - Declare a winner for specific use cases. Never "det afhænger af" without resolution.
   - Format: "Vil du have [X], vælg [Product A]. Har du brug for [Y], er [Product B] svaret."
   - Affiliate links on ALL product names in this section.
   - End with an active CTA - link to the recommended product or invite the reader to compare prices.

### Affiliate links
- At least 2 affiliate links must appear before the 500-word mark (CRO). Subsequent product affiliate links can fall later in the article.
- Every pricerunner.dk URL must have ?refsite=adrunner_dk_husforbegyndere.

### SEO
- Focus keyword: "[Category] sammenligning" or "[Product A] vs [Product B]"
- SEO title: 50-60 chars, contains both product names or brands + "vs" or "sammenligning"
- SEO slug: "[product-a]-vs-[product-b]" (lowercase, hyphenated)
- featured_image_product_id: first product in brief.products

### Tone
Neutral and fact-driven in the body sections. Opinionated and direct in the conclusion.
"Du"-form. The reader trusts you to cut through the marketing - deliver a real verdict.

### What to avoid
- Favouring one product in the body sections (save opinion for conclusion)
- Vague pros like "god ydelse" - every claim needs a spec or number
- Wishy-washy conclusion ("begge er gode valg") - always name a winner per use case
- Missing comparison table - it is mandatory
- "I dette udvalg", "i denne sammenligning" as opener phrases
- Passive CTA - always use an active click verb
- "ligeledes", "endvidere", "desuden" as sentence starters
