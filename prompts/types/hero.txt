## Type-specific instructions: hero

articleType in brief is "hero". There are 5-10 products in context.brief.products.
This is a roundup guide — the reader is at the top of the funnel, comparing the whole
category. They need a buying guide AND specific recommendations. You serve both.

### Target word count
1500-2500 words total. Aim for 1700-2000. The minimum is enforced from brief.writing_rules.min_words.
Longer is fine if every section earns its space — no padding, no restating.

### Required structure

0. H1 (MANDATORY — first line of article)
   Format: # Bedste [category] [year] — [one-line angle]
   The year is brief.article_hook's year. Category name comes from brief.category.
   Example: # Bedste robotplæneklipper 2026 — sådan vælger du den rette til din have
   Must contain "bedste" and the category name.

1. Introduction (100-150 words)
   - Frame the problem: what makes this category hard to choose in, why this guide exists.
   - Name your top pick and at least one runner-up.
   - FIRST sentence MUST contain a markdown affiliate link on one of the top products.
   - Tell the reader what they get from this guide.
   - Do NOT mention review counts, watcher counts, or number of shops.

2. Vores anbefalinger på 30 sekunder (50-100 words, H2: "Vores anbefalinger på 30 sekunder")
   - Quick-glance bucket recommendations with affiliate links. Pick 3-4 buckets that fit the brief.
   - Format each as a single line: "**Bedst overall**: [Product](affiliateUrl) — kort begrundelse."
   - Bucket suggestions (choose what makes sense for the products in the brief):
     - Bedst overall
     - Bedst budget / billigst
     - Bedst premium
     - Bedst til [specific use case based on category]
     - Bedst til begyndere
   - Every product mentioned here MUST be linked. No bare names.

3. Sådan vælger du den rette [category] (200-300 words, H2 must start with "Sådan vælger" or contain "vælg")
   - Mandatory buying guide section. This is what lets the article rank for informational queries.
   - 3-5 evaluation criteria as a bullet list. Each bullet: criterion name (bold) + 1-2 sentences of explanation.
   - Be category-specific. For robotplæneklippere: havestørrelse, kantklipning, GPS vs. kantledning, vejrtæthed, støjniveau.
     For varmepumper: COP-værdi, kapacitet i kW, støj udedel, vinterdrift.
     For espressomaskiner: bartryk, vandtank, mælkeskummer, kværn.
   - Do not list specs as criteria. List decisions the reader has to make.
   - No affiliate links in this section. Pure guidance.

4. Per-product sections (5-10 products, 120-180 words each)
   Heading format: ## [N]. [Product name] — [bucket label]
   Where [bucket label] is chosen by you based on the product's spec/price position in the brief.
   At least 2 of your N product headings MUST contain "Bedst" (e.g. "Bedst i test", "Bedst budget", "Bedst til store haver", "Bedst på pris").
   Other headings may use editorial framing without "Bedst" (e.g. "Premium-valget", "Det stille valg").

   Each product section contains:
   - First sentence: strongest argument (spec-backed from brief). Product name as a markdown link.
   - 2-3 bullets with concrete specs. Each bullet: spec + why it matters in practice. No vague "god ydelse".
   - One honest weakness (REAL product limitation, not meta-comment).
   - Closing line: "Passer bedst til: [specific buyer profile]."
   - Image anchor: after-heading on this section's H2.
   - Widget anchor: end-of-section on this section's H2.

   Numbering: prefix the heading with the rank number (1., 2., ...). The numbering reflects
   editorial preference, not raw popularity from the brief — you decide the order. The
   first product in your section list should match the "Bedst overall" pick.

5. Sammenligning på et øjeblik (H2: "Sammenligning på et øjeblik")
   - Markdown table with one row per product (max 10 rows).
   - Columns: Pris, then 2-3 key specs that differ between products, then Bedst til.
   - Scannable. No prose in the table cells.
   - This section has no image or widget anchor.

6. Vores anbefaling (H2: "Vores anbefaling" or "Hvad skal du vælge?" — pick one that fits)
   - 100-150 words. The verdict section.
   - State a clear recommendation per use case: "Vil du have X, vælg [Product]. Har du brug for Y, er [Product] svaret."
   - The top 3 products in brief.products MUST appear as affiliate markdown links in this section.
   - End with an active CTA: "Se aktuelle priser ->" or "Sammenlign dine favoritter her ->".

7. Ofte stillede spørgsmål (H2: "Ofte stillede spørgsmål")
   - 3-5 questions. Each question as H3, answer in 2-4 sentences directly below.
   - Questions must be category-specific and target real search queries people ask.
     Good question types:
     - "Hvad er den bedste [category] til [specific use case]?" — name the winner + brief why
     - "Hvad koster en [category]?" — give a price range from the brief, low to high
     - "Hvad skal jeg kigge efter når jeg køber en [category]?" — 2-3 key criteria, brief
     - "Er [brand] [category] gode?" — direct answer, balanced
     - "Hvor længe holder batteriet på en [category]?" — category-specific spec question
   - Answers are informational. No affiliate links in this section.
   - No meta-commentary ("det er et godt spørgsmål"). Answer directly.
   - This section has no image or widget anchor.

### Affiliate links
- At least 2 affiliate links must appear in the introduction or "Vores anbefalinger på 30 sekunder" section combined.
- Every product mentioned in the "Vores anbefalinger på 30 sekunder" section MUST be linked.
- Every pricerunner.dk URL must contain ?refsite=adrunner_dk_husforbegyndere.

### SEO
- Focus keyword: "bedste [category]" (e.g. "bedste robotplæneklipper") — drop the year for the focus_keyword field.
- SEO title: 50-60 chars, contains "bedste" + category name. Include the year if it fits.
- SEO slug: "bedste-[category]" or "bedste-[category]-[year]" (lowercase, hyphenated).
- featured_image_product_id: id of the "Bedst overall" pick (your top product, usually brief.products[0]).

### Tone
Authoritative buying-guide voice. First-person plural ("vi anbefaler", "vores favorit").
Direct and decisive — readers came here for a recommendation, not hedging. Warmth is good
in the buying guide section; sharpness is right in the verdict.

IMPORTANT: You write from product specifications in the brief — NOT from personal testing.
Never claim to have tested, owned, or used any product over time. Specs argue the case.
You frame and rank them based on the brief data.

### What to avoid
- Missing buying guide section — "Sådan vælger du..." is mandatory
- Missing FAQ section — "Ofte stillede spørgsmål" is mandatory
- Missing bucket labels — at least 2 product headings must contain "Bedst"
- Fewer than 5 product sections — minimum is 5
- Missing comparison table
- Wishy-washy verdict ("alle er gode valg") — name winners by use case
- Vague pros like "god ydelse" — every claim needs a spec or number
- Passive CTA in verdict — always use an active click verb
- Generic buying criteria that could apply to any category — be specific to this category
- Numbering products by raw "popularity rank" from brief — editorial order is your call
- Review counts, watcher counts, or PriceRunner name in body text
- AI hedge phrases: "Det er alligevel rimeligt at nævne", "man kan argumentere for"
- "ligeledes", "endvidere", "desuden" as sentence starters
- ASCII substitutes for Danish characters (aa→å, ae→æ, oe→ø) — always use the real characters
