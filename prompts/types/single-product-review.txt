## Type-specific instructions: single-product-review

articleType in brief is "single-product-review". There is exactly one product. This is a
standalone review — opinionated, thorough, and personal.

### Target word count
700-1000 words total. Aim for 750+ to give a comfortable buffer above the 700-word minimum.

### Required structure

0. H1 heading (MANDATORY — first line of article)
   Format: # [Product name] anmeldelse — [one-line verdict or hook]
   Example: # Dreame X50 Ultra Kombi Sort anmeldelse — er det en robot, der holder hvad den lover?
   Must contain the product name and "anmeldelse" or "test".

1. Introduction (80-120 words)
   - FIRST sentence MUST contain the product name as a markdown link:
     [Product Name](affiliateUrl)
     Do NOT write the product name in bold (**...**) — it must be a hyperlink.
     Example: Vi har kigget nærmere på [Segway Navimow i105E](https://www.pricerunner.dk/...)...
   - Hook with the product's defining characteristic — price, a key spec, or build quality.
     Do NOT mention review counts, watcher counts, or number of shops. These say nothing
     about the product and make the intro feel like a data dump.

2. Hvad er det? (80-150 words, H2)
   - Brief overview: category, price, key specs from brief.
   - Who it is designed for — be specific, not "den travle husstand".
   - Image placement anchor goes here: after-heading "Hvad er det?"

3. Det vi kan lide (150-200 words, H2: "Det vi kan lide")
   - Bullet list with 4-5 concrete pros (spec-backed, from brief).
   - Each bullet: one thing + why it matters in practice. No vague "god ydelse".
   - Short conversational paragraph after the list.

4. Det vi ville ændre (80-120 words, H2: "Det vi ville ændre")
   - Bullet list with 2-3 honest cons — REAL product limitations, not meta-comments.
   - No sugarcoating. A review with no cons loses credibility.

5. Hvem er den til? (60-100 words, H2: "Hvem er den til?")
   - "Koeb den hvis..." — specific reason with data.
   - "Overvej [specific named alternative] hvis..." — NAME the alternative explicitly. You may link it to its PriceRunner product page, but ONLY if you append "?refsite=adrunner_dk_husforbegyndere" to the URL. Never use a bare PriceRunner URL without this ref — it earns nothing.
   - Widget placement anchor goes here: end-of-section "Hvem er den til?"

6. Vores vurdering (H2: "Vores vurdering", 60-100 words)
   - MANDATORY: include a numerical rating using exactly this format: "Vi giver den **X ud af 5**" where X is a whole number 1-5.
   - Rating calibration: 5/5 means near-perfect with no meaningful cons. If "Det vi ville ændre"
     lists 2+ real cons, the rating must be 4 or lower. A product with genuine limitations cannot
     score 5/5 — that would contradict the review itself and kill reader trust.
   - Affiliate link on product name.
   - Active CTA at the end: "Se aktuel pris ->" or "Tjek prisen her ->". No shop counts.

### Tone
First-person plural and opinionated. Warmth is good; false enthusiasm kills trust.
IMPORTANT: You write from product specifications in the brief — NOT from personal testing.
Never claim to have used, tested, owned, or experienced the product over time.
Write "Batteritiden på 220 min rækker til store boliger" not "Vi testede den over en uge".
Specs argue the case. Your voice frames and interprets them.

### What to avoid
- Missing H1 — every article MUST start with a # heading
- Vague pros like "god ydelse" — every claim needs a spec or concrete reason
- "I dette udvalg", "i denne anmeldelse" as opening phrases
- Artificial hedging — take a position
- "Overve j alternativet" without naming the alternative — always name it explicitly
- Meta-cons like "begrænset specifikationsdata" — only real product limitations
- Passive CTA in verdict — always use an active click verb
- Review counts, watcher counts, or number of shops in the article body
- AI hedge phrases: "Det er alligevel rimeligt at nævne", "man kan argumentere for"
- "ligeledes", "endvidere", "desuden" as sentence starters
- ASCII substitutes for Danish characters (aa→å, ae→æ, oe→ø) — always use the real characters
